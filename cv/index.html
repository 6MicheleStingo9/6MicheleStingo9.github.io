Professional Summary

As a Ph.D. in Computational Linguistics, my expertise lies at the intersection of Natural Language Processing, Large Language Models, and the design of intelligent agent-based systems. My current research focuses on the development of cognitive agents and multi-agent architectures, with particular attention to orchestration mechanisms, stateful reasoning, and coordination patterns implemented through frameworks such as LangGraph. A central component of my research concerns knowledge representation and reasoning, particularly the use of ontologies and graph-based models to structure and organize information within intelligent systems. I have practical experience with tools such as Protégé and Neo4j, and I explore how formal knowledge structures can enhance the capabilities of LLM-based architectures. These concepts increasingly inform my work on agent memory, shared state management, and symbolic–subsymbolic integration, enabling the design of systems in which language models interact with structured knowledge to support more reliable reasoning and decision-making. I specialize in the use of advanced prompt engineering techniques to fully leverage the reasoning, planning, and interaction capabilities of modern language models. I design prompts and control flows that support complex decision-making processes, tool usage, and inter-agent communication. My work actively contributes to transforming LLMs from simple text generators into goal-oriented components within structured systems. My previous experience includes the development of conversational systems using platforms such as IBM WatsonX and Azure Cognitive Services, providing a strong foundation in production-oriented AI solutions. I actively explore and evaluate emerging frameworks and tools for LLM-based applications, including LangChain, LangGraph, Ollama, and the Hugging Face Transformers ecosystem. My work is guided by continuous research into new scientific directions, architectural patterns, and methodologies aimed at building robust, interpretable, and scalable intelligent systems.
